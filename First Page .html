LEGISLATURE This branch makes the laws. It is like a big group of elected people who come together to decide on rules everyone has to follow. info

The Legislature in India is a bicameral body comprising the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States). The Lok Sabha is the lower house, with members elected directly by the people through general elections. It is responsible for making laws, scrutinizing government policies, and representing public interests. The Rajya Sabha is the upper house, with members appointed by state legislatures and nominated by the President. It reviews and revises legislation proposed by the Lok Sabha. Together, both houses of the Legislature debate and pass laws, hold the Executive accountable, and play a critical role in shaping national policy.

EXECUTIVE This branch enforces the laws. It is led by the President or Prime Minister, who ensures that the laws are followed and that the government runs smoothly. info

In India, the Executive branch is responsible for implementing and enforcing laws. It is headed by the President, who is the ceremonial head of state, while the real executive power lies with the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers. The Prime Minister, as the head of government, leads the executive branch, making key policy decisions and overseeing government operations. The Executive's responsibilities include administering government programs, formulating policies, and representing India in foreign affairs. It ensures that laws passed by the Legislature are executed and maintains the day-to-day functions of the government, playing a vital role in national administration.

JUDICIARY This branch interprets the laws. It includes judges and courts, who decide what the laws mean and make sure they are applied fairly. info

In India, the Judiciary is a cornerstone of the democratic system, tasked with upholding the Constitution and ensuring justice. It comprises the Supreme Court, High Courts, and subordinate courts. The Supreme Court, the highest judicial authority, oversees constitutional interpretation and protects fundamental rights. High Courts handle state-level legal matters and supervise subordinate courts. This multi-tiered structure allows for thorough legal scrutiny and appeals. The Judiciary in India also plays a crucial role in reviewing the actions of the Legislature and Executive, ensuring that governance adheres to constitutional principles. By safeguarding rights and ensuring legal accountability, the Indian Judiciary upholds the rule of law and democracy.
